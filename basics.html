Basics Of Stock Market

An index option is a financial derivative that gives the holder the right (but not the obligation) to buy or sell the value of an underlying index, such as the S&P 500 index, at the stated exercise price. ... Often, an index option will utilize an index futures contract as its underlying asset.


A stock option is a contract between two parties that gives the buyer the right to buy or sell underlying stocks. An individual who owns stock in a company is called a shareholder and is eligible to claim part of the company's residual assets and earnings (should the company ever be dissolved).
